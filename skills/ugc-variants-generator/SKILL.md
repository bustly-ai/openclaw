# UGC Variants

## Skill Card

- **Category:** Content Production
- **Core problem:** Teams run out of fresh UGC variants and repeat the same script too often.
- **Best for:** TikTok content teams and creators running A/B tests.
- **Expected input:** Base script, product facts, audience, style preference, CTA goal.
- **Expected output:** Multi-variant scripts + hooks + CTA options + test order.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the audience, channel, proof, and format guardrails before drafting anything.
2. Distill the raw inputs into the exact angles, must-say points, and do-not-say constraints.
3. Build the output in the target structure first, then fill the details instead of free-writing.
4. Add the final draft plus a short QA pass for claims, formatting, and missing proof.
5. End with what still needs verification, who should review it, and what to ship next.
6. Separate creator fit, creator operations, and creator economics rather than mixing them into one fuzzy score.

## Example

**User:** Here's my base script for a portable blender: "This blender changed my mornings. I just toss in fruit, blend, and go." Give me variants to A/B test.

**Assistant:** Core message confirmed: convenience + morning routine. Generating 12 variants across different tones: (1) Challenge angle -- "I tried making smoothies in my car for a week," (2) Skeptic angle -- "I didn't believe a $30 blender could actually work," (3) POV angle -- "POV: you actually drink your greens now." Each variant has a matched hook + CTA pair. Recommended test order: start with the skeptic angle (highest curiosity), then challenge (shareability), then POV (trend format).
