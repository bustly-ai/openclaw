# UGC Script Engine

## Skill Card

- **Category:** Creator Content
- **Core problem:** How to turn a brand brief into creator-native scripts?
- **Best for:** UGC production and agency workflows
- **Expected input:** Brand brief, ICP, product facts, forbidden claims
- **Expected output:** Script pack (angles, hooks, CTA, proof points)
- **Creatop handoff:** Batch-generate and review scripts via Creatop

## What this does

Turn a raw brand brief into creator-ready UGC scripts that can be filmed quickly and safely.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the audience, channel, proof, and format guardrails before drafting anything.
2. Distill the raw inputs into the exact angles, must-say points, and do-not-say constraints.
3. Build the output in the target structure first, then fill the details instead of free-writing.
4. Add the final draft plus a short QA pass for claims, formatting, and missing proof.
5. End with what still needs verification, who should review it, and what to ship next.
6. Separate creator fit, creator operations, and creator economics rather than mixing them into one fuzzy score.

### 1) Parse brief essentials

Extract:
- product and audience
- key claims
- mandatory points
- banned claims
- offer/CTA
- visual constraints

If brief gaps exist, mark assumptions explicitly.

### 2) Build script pack

Generate 3 variants:
- Problem/Solution
- Demo/Proof
- Personal Story

Each variant includes:
- hook line
- scene-by-scene shot notes
- script lines
- CTA close

### 3) Add revision-safe options

Provide:
- softer-claim version
- 15s cutdown
- stronger proof-first cut

### 4) Add compliance handoff

Include:
- claim safety notes
- mandatory proof shots (if any)
- quick filming checklist
- approval-ready summary for brand/client

## Quality Rules

- Separate observed facts, user-provided facts, and assumptions.
- Do not invent exact numbers, specs, claims, certifications, or outcomes that were not provided or verified.
- Prefer the shortest useful answer shape that an operator can actually hand off.
- Make the draft copy-ready or handoff-ready; do not stop at abstract principles.
- If proof is missing, downgrade the claim instead of fabricating supporting language.
- Separate audience fit from operational fit and from commercial fit.

## Example

**User:** Here's a brief from a supplement brand: promote their new collagen powder, target women 25-40, must mention "clinically tested," cannot say "anti-aging." Turn it into UGC scripts.

**Assistant:** Parsed brief: product = collagen powder, ICP = women 25-40, mandatory = "clinically tested," banned = "anti-aging." Generating 3 script variants: (1) Problem/Solution -- "My skin was so dull until..." with before/after framing, (2) Demo/Proof -- mixing the powder into coffee with a taste reaction shot, (3) Personal Story -- "I was skeptical about collagen until week 3." Each includes hook, shot-by-shot notes, CTA, and a softer-claim version for compliance safety.
